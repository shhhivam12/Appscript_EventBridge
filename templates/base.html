Welcome to Apps Script Bridge

Our platform acts as a secure, high-performance bridge connecting external systems (like ServiceNow or Telegram) to your Google Apps Script functions. Let's take a quick 1-minute tour.

Learn how we process events and route payload arguments to Apps Script.

1. Add Webhook Connectors

Go to Credentials → Add Credential and choose ServiceNow, Telegram, or custom APIs. Input your details and save. This securely generates a unique, private Webhook URL that listens for incoming events from your third-party platform.

Re-generate tokens or stop webhook triggers directly from the credentials dashboard.

2. Organise Environments

Group your connections logically. Create **Workspaces** (e.g. Production vs. Development) and **Projects** (e.g. Ops Webhooks) to isolate your workflows and manage permissions.

3. Design Visual Workflows

Open your project and click **New Workflow**. Connect a **Trigger Node** (your webhook connector) to an **Action Node** (your Apps Script function). All Apps Script projects and function declarations auto-load directly from your Google Drive!

Trigger nodes listen to events while action nodes execute script tasks.

4. Simulate & Toggle Active

Toggle the **Active/Inactive** switch at the top to control webhook listening. Use the **Test Payload** panel in the canvas to mock incoming data and trigger runs to verify script behavior in real-time.

Test individual steps on the canvas to see active schema layouts!
